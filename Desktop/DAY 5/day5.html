SUPER ENERGY

BMW's approach to energy encompasses its electromobility strategy, which includes developing efficient electric vehicles and using their batteries for energy storage and grid stabilization, as well as its sustainable production methods. The company focuses on creating fully electric vehicles and integrating them into a holistic energy ecosystem through innovations like Vehicle-to-Grid (V2G) technology, renewable energy sourcing for production, and large-scale battery storage facilities.

SPEED RIDING

Top Speed: Top speeds are typically electronically limited to around \(250\) km/h or \(305\) km/h for some models.
Suspension and Brakes: To handle the high speeds, these models feature specialized suspension and braking systems, often with M-specific components for enhanced performance and durability.

ENGINE

These cars feature powerful engines, often with turbochargers. For example, the M8 Competition uses a \(625\) hp, twin-turbo V8, while the M4 CS has a twin-turbo inline-six.
Acceleration: The M8 Competition can go from \(0-100\) km/h in \(3.2\) seconds, while the M4 CS does it in \(3.4\) seconds.
