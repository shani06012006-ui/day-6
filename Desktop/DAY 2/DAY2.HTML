A coffee house is a public establishment that specializes in serving coffee, tea, and other light refreshments. Historically, in 17th and 18th century England, they were vital social hubs where men from all strata of society gathered to discuss news, politics, literature, and business, earning them the nickname "schools of wisdom". For the price of a penny, a customer could buy a cup of coffee and gain admission to a world of lively conversation and information exchange.
